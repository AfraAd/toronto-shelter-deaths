Key Insights & Dataset

COVID-19 Impact (2020-2024)

The data reveals a dramatic surge in shelter resident deaths beginning in 2020, coinciding with the COVID-19 pandemic. This period represents the most significant increase in mortality rates throughout the entire dataset.

Historical Context

Prior to 2020, shelter deaths remained relatively stable with gradual fluctuations. The pre-pandemic period (2007-2019) established a baseline that makes the subsequent spike particularly striking.

Ongoing Crisis

Deaths have remained elevated through 2024, indicating that the factors contributing to increased mortality - whether related to pandemic aftermath, housing instability, or healthcare access - continue to affect Toronto's vulnerable shelter population.
